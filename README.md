# Palette
## A computing framework for single-cell multimodal data mosaic integration
![Overview of Palette framework](Palette_overview.png)

The growing availability of single-cell multimodal datasets enables the construction of comprehensive cellular atlases, presenting a challenging integration scenario, namely mosaic integration, where modality composition varies across batches. Such integration is complicated by two key factors: batch effects within individual modalities and heterogeneous modality composition across batches, which jointly obscure biological signals and hinder effective data integration. Here, we introduce Palette, a flexible and interpretable framework for integrating single-cell multimodal data. Palette builds on a variant of principal component analysis to disentangle technical noise and biological variations, and leverages the topological structure of the data to accommodate imbalanced modality composition. Across a range of bi- and trimodal mosaic integration tasks, Palette consistently outperforms state-of-the-art mosaic integration algorithms in biological conservation and batch correction, while robustly mixing batches with various modality compositions. Notably, we show the application of Palette on the cross-condition and cross-species datasets, effectively preserving meaningful biological signals. Finally, we demonstrate that Palette extends beyond single-cell mosaic integration to accommodate other challenging scenarios. Together, these results position Palette as a robust and versatile framework for harmonizing complex multimodal datasets and facilitating their joint analysis.
